Immune regulation and tolerance

Our immune systems are multilevel, organized ecosystems, comprising a multitude of cell types that engage in competitive and co-operative interactions to elicit a stunningly rich and dynamics response against infections and tumors. Primarily, distinct subtypes of T and B lymphocytes act in synchrony to clear the pathogen (effector response) and protect against future exposures (memory response). The dynamics of T and B cells within our body and their responsiveness is tightly regulated to conserve energy and space, and to prevent attack on our own cells (auto-immune conditions). The central aim of this project is to understand the mechanisms that govern lymphocyte distribution and functionality either as means of controlling an effector response or tolerating cells from our own body (immune-tolerance).
